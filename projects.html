PROJECT CELER

Apr 2020 - Dec 2020


Connected traffic system.


Celer is a traffic simulation that was made in Python using a combination of Top Down and Object Oriented programming. It includes pathfinding, scheduling, and clustering algorithms, that were used in combination with NYC data to make the system come to life.

As project lead, I came up with the idea and did most of the research needed to implement such a project. I created multiple project documents and coded a sizeable portion of the simulation.



CSC290 Blog

Sep 2020


Fully responsive personal blog.


Fully responsive blog implemented in Flask for my Communication Skills for Computer Scientists course. The blog is fully expandable supporting multiple authors and an infinite amount of posts (as long as GitHub storage permits).

All the posts are stored in the "posts" subdirectory as .txt files, which are parsed by the website to update the blog with the newest content. The website also includes an algorithm to calculate reading time based on average reading speeds.



CAPTAIN TRACKER

Aug 2019


A website where people can search for their fortnite stats.


Captain Tracker is a website made in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, where players can compare statistics as well as share them with friends. Players can also see all the latest in-game news and Fortnite Tweets. On top of all that, they can see the current item shop and customize messages to send to their friends.

I was the UI designer and programmer who made all the features of this website.



shsHacks

Sep 2018 - Mar 2019


The FIRST EVER Stevenson High School Hackathon!


As a board member of Computer Science Club at Stevenson High School in the 2018-2019 school year, I set out to organize our school's first ever hackathon (24 hour coding competition). I worked to contact potential sponsors and manage our budget, as well as speaking with teachers to convince them to chaperone the event. During the event, I collaborated with the other organizers to manage the event and make sure everything was going smoothly by helping with the sign-in of participants and meal distribution throughout the 24 hour period.

The event was a success, with over 100 participants and over $9,000 in prizes, which were given to the top 3 teams, selected by judges (professionals provided by sponsors) who picked them based on a series of criteria devised by my team and I.
