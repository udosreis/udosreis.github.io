Public Transit System

Nov 2020 - Dec 2020


System to manage public transit transactions.


This project was created for my Software Design class and was implemented in a team of 4 using Agile Development. It is fully programmed in Java.

PTS is a public transit card system that manages customer transit cards and transactions. The project has an admin and customer side. The admins can create customer accounts as well as suspend customer transit cards or accounts. Admins can also view different statistics such as revenue during a certain time frame, revenue by bus or train station, view the amount of traffic at each station, and other important metrics.

On the other hand, customers can add cards to their account as well as fund their cards. They can also suspend their cards in case they lose them. Other than that, customers can tap in and out of stations with their cards to automatically deduct the transit fee from their account. This fee is calculated using the tap in and out locations.

I was responsible for parts of the cardholder and admin classes as well as the cardholder side of the CLI.

Mental Drive

November 2017


An iOS app to help those with mental disorders.


MentalDrive was made during HuskieHack at Northern Illinois university. It was programmed in Swift in a team of 4. The project won the "My Own World" award (equivalent to first place).

MentalDrive is an all-in-one app to help monitor someone with a mental disability. Based on research conducted, having a set structure is crucially important for those living with a mental disability, so my team and I created a scheduling feature to make sure that all tasks are completed on time and in an orderly function. To add on to this, many mentally ill patients are on multiple, sometimes several medications. We set up our app to where the user can always keep a schedule of when to take their medications. Another feature the app utilizes is the behavior log, which is preset with the most common poor behaviors that are the product of a wild variety of mental diseases, including but not limited to Aspberger's Disease, Opioid addiction, and Bipolar disorder. The behavior log will allow the guardian to give a description of what happened before and after the behavior taking place. The relaxation section is the perfect place for when a patient gets overwhelmed and they need a place to go to get calmed down. It has a music player, meditation assistant, and a game. We also have an emotion log. This will give the patient a set of emoticons and they will select which one represents their mood at a given time. It also has speech-to-text capabilities so they can learn and practice actually saying how they are feeling.

As part of the team, I worked on the design of the app as well as the home page, relaxation page, and about page.
